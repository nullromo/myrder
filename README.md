# Myrder

Website for a shared-deck Magic the Gathering format. Check out
[myrder.com](myrder.com).

## original primer

**Maybeboard** Cards that I thought about including but haven't.

- [[Ebony Charm]], [[Fade from Memory]], [[Rapid Decay]], [[Scarab Feast]] -
  Spells that exile cards from the graveyard. It would be bad to include too
  many of these because exiling all the [[Myr Servitors]] makes it hard for the
  game to actually end.
- [[Lantern of the Lost]], [[Nihil Spellbomb]], [[Jack-o'-Lantern]],
  [[Sentinel Totem]], [[Thran Foundry]], [[Tormod's Crypt]],
  [[Soul-Guide Lantern]] - Artifacts that exile cards from the graveyard. Same
  rationale as above, and including zero of these makes the decklist a little
  cleaner with the "no artifacts" rule.
- [[Dredge]] - Pretty cool, but just a little too weak.
- [[Mire's Toll]], [[Dread Fugue]], [[Duress]] - Hand disruption. These are fair
  1-for-1s, but having too many in the list wouldn't be good. Disrupting
  someone's only source of draw can snowball the game.
- [[Skulltap]], [[Merciless Resolve]] - More versions of cards that are in the
  list already.
- [[Poison the Waters]] - Another wrath.

**Exclusions** Cards that wre intentionally excluded or that were removed.

- [[Sign in Blood]], [[Night's Whisper]], [[Corpse Churn]],
  [[Foreboding Fruit]], [[Infernal Contract]], [[Atrocious Experiment]]
  (removed), [[Cruel Bargain]], etc. - Unconditional draw spells.
- [[Titan's Presence]] - Neat spell but just too clunky to use.
- [[Bankrupt in Blood]] (removed) - Too tricky to use and doesn't provide enough
  extra wow factor over other similar cards.
- [[Darksteel Citadel]] - Kinda cool but colorless mana hurts a lot when most of
  the spells cost 1-2 mana.
- [[Guildless Commons]], [[Arid Archway]] - Neat forms of card advantage that
  can even pick up cycling/spell lands, but the spells in the deck are so black
  mana intensive that these don't really work and they feel bad to draw.
- [[Agonizing Memories]] (removed) - Manipulates the top of the deck and
  provides card advantage, but costs 4 mana so it doesn't play well.
- [[Cling to Dust]] (removed) - Extremely powerful repeatable removal, card
  draw, life gain, etc. This card does everything and it does way too much of
  it.
- [[Mogis's Favor]] - Non-exile targeted removal, but it's repeatable. Since the
  escape cost is only 2 cards, this one would probably get out of hand. After
  seeing how [[Cling to Dust]] played, I don't think this one would be fair.

**Changelog**

_02 Dec 2025_

- Removed [[Cling to Dust]] After playing 1 game with this card is was very
  obvious that it shouldn't be in the decklist. It is very easy to fill up the
  graveyard in this format, so you can pay the escape cost 2-5 times over the
  course of the game. It's also an instant, making it very hard to remove with
  graveyard hate. It draws cards, it exiles stuff, it gains life, it's nuts.
- Added [[Nasty End]] to provide more card draw, which I had previously cut down
  a little and was worried about.

_20 Nov 2025_

- Swapped [[Poison the Waters]] out for [[Bile Blight because having an instant
  speed wrath seemed cool. You can get blown out with it though, so it might not
  play very well.
- Swapped one [[Swamp]] out for [[Blasted Landscape]] to reduce flood.
- Removed [[Agonizing Memories]] for being too clunky.
- Removed [[Atrocious Experiment]] for violating the "no unconditional card
  draw" rule.
- Removed 1 [[Swamp]] because the average mana cost of the deck decreased and it
  felt a little land-heavy.
- Removed [[Bankrupt in Blood]] for being too clunky.
- Added [[Inquisition of Kozilek]] because it seems interesting. I really prefer
  the 7th edition [[Duress]] artwork, and they're nearly the same card, but I
  wanted to open up the line of discarding a myr to exile or reanimate it.
- Added a small [[Entomb]] package ([[Coffin Purge]] and [[Cling to Dust]]). I
  worry that [[Cling to Dust]] is too powerful because once you have it in your
  graveyard you can get a lot of uses out of it. It's there as a test for now.
- Keeping an eye on [[Haunting Misery]]. Some players found it to be an un-fun
  way for the game to end. It pretty much does nothing until you just win with
  it. I expect that experienced players can play around it more easily, but it
  may be worth taking out just to remove the "oops, you're dead" aspect.
- Worried I went a little too light on the draw spells, but we will see over
  time.
