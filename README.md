# Myrder

Website for a shared-deck Magic the Gathering format. Check out
[myrder.com](myrder.com).

## original primer

Original list by catdaddy360: moxfield.com/decks/U10Q_zEz-0m2EQOEpdPR4Q

Myrder (so-called because the original creator pronounces "myr" like "mrr" so the name sounds like "Murder") focuses on [[Myr Servitor]] as a unique shared resource that can be easily exploited by both players. 8 copies of [[Reanimate]] provide a push and pull to the gameplay which makes it really feel like you are fighting over the same pool of resources. The format is all about tricky little edges you can find with the cards, and since all the spells are so cheap you get to cast a lot of them over the course of the game. Paying close attention to which cards are instants will benefit you, as there are a number of cute interactions that can occur throughout the deck.

_Also check out my Forgetful Fish list: deckstats.net/decks/102881/3247652-kyle-s-forgetful-fish_

**Rules**

- This is intended as a 1v1 format.
- Starting life total is 10.
- The library is shared, but graveyards are not.
- The owner of a card on the battlefield is the player who put it onto the battlefield.
- You can decide the mulligan rules on your own :)

**Decklist Rationale**
This list was created using the following guiding principles. There may be some bends or breaks in these rules, but for the most part these are the rules.

- (Mandatory) There can be no creatures nor creature tokens other than [[Myr Servitor]].
- Targeted removal spells should exile their target (so that they actually do something).
- Mass removal spells should not exile (so that they don't do too much).
- Draw spells should always come with a game-state condition (other than just losing life).
- Do not include any double-faced cards.
- Do not include any permanents that sit on the battlefield forever other than lands and [[Myr Servitor]]s. Basically this means no artifacts or enchantments.

**Individual Card Notes**
Playing the game and learning all the neat little interactions is part of the fun. However, I will mention some of the more commonly missed things.

- [[Vault of Whispers]] is an artifact. Some spells let you sacrifice artifacts.
- Read [[Scene of the Crime]] carefully. Notice anything missing? Here's a clue: look for a tap symbol in the last ability. Didn't find it? Hmm...
- [[Cannibalize]] has a weird trick where if one of the targets doesn't exist by the time it resolves, you can just do one of the things. For instance, if you cast it targeting your own creatures, you can sacrifice one in response and just get the [[Cannibalize]] counters on the other. If you cast it targeting your opponent's creatures, you can exile one in response and just do the [[Cannibalize]] exile.

**Maybeboard**
Cards that I thought about including but haven't.

- [[Ebony Charm]], [[Fade from Memory]], [[Rapid Decay]], [[Scarab Feast]] - Spells that exile cards from the graveyard. It would be bad to include too many of these because exiling all the [[Myr Servitors]] makes it hard for the game to actually end.
- [[Lantern of the Lost]], [[Nihil Spellbomb]], [[Jack-o'-Lantern]], [[Sentinel Totem]], [[Thran Foundry]], [[Tormod's Crypt]], [[Soul-Guide Lantern]] - Artifacts that exile cards from the graveyard. Same rationale as above, and including zero of these makes the decklist a little cleaner with the "no artifacts" rule.
- [[Dredge]] - Pretty cool, but just a little too weak.
- [[Mire's Toll]], [[Dread Fugue]], [[Duress]] - Hand disruption. These are fair 1-for-1s, but having too many in the list wouldn't be good. Disrupting someone's only source of draw can snowball the game.
- [[Skulltap]], [[Merciless Resolve]] - More versions of cards that are in the list already.
- [[Poison the Waters]] - Another wrath.

**Exclusions**
Cards that wre intentionally excluded or that were removed.

- [[Sign in Blood]], [[Night's Whisper]], [[Corpse Churn]], [[Foreboding Fruit]], [[Infernal Contract]], [[Atrocious Experiment]] (removed), [[Cruel Bargain]], etc. - Unconditional draw spells.
- [[Titan's Presence]] - Neat spell but just too clunky to use.
- [[Bankrupt in Blood]] (removed) - Too tricky to use and doesn't provide enough extra wow factor over other similar cards.
- [[Darksteel Citadel]] - Kinda cool but colorless mana hurts a lot when most of the spells cost 1-2 mana.
- [[Guildless Commons]], [[Arid Archway]] - Neat forms of card advantage that can even pick up cycling/spell lands, but the spells in the deck are so black mana intensive that these don't really work and they feel bad to draw.
- [[Agonizing Memories]] (removed) - Manipulates the top of the deck and provides card advantage, but costs 4 mana so it doesn't play well.
- [[Cling to Dust]] (removed) - Extremely powerful repeatable removal, card draw, life gain, etc. This card does everything and it does way too much of it.
- [[Mogis's Favor]] - Non-exile targeted removal, but it's repeatable. Since the escape cost is only 2 cards, this one would probably get out of hand. After seeing how [[Cling to Dust]] played, I don't think this one would be fair.

**Changelog**

_02 Dec 2025_

- Removed [[Cling to Dust]] After playing 1 game with this card is was very obvious that it shouldn't be in the decklist. It is very easy to fill up the graveyard in this format, so you can pay the escape cost 2-5 times over the course of the game. It's also an instant, making it very hard to remove with graveyard hate. It draws cards, it exiles stuff, it gains life, it's nuts.
- Added [[Nasty End]] to provide more card draw, which I had previously cut down a little and was worried about.

_20 Nov 2025_

- Swapped [[Poison the Waters]] out for [[Bile Blight because having an instant speed wrath seemed cool. You can get blown out with it though, so it might not play very well.
- Swapped one [[Swamp]] out for [[Blasted Landscape]] to reduce flood.
- Removed [[Agonizing Memories]] for being too clunky.
- Removed [[Atrocious Experiment]] for violating the "no unconditional card draw" rule.
- Removed 1 [[Swamp]] because the average mana cost of the deck decreased and it felt a little land-heavy.
- Removed [[Bankrupt in Blood]] for being too clunky.
- Added [[Inquisition of Kozilek]] because it seems interesting. I really prefer the 7th edition [[Duress]] artwork, and they're nearly the same card, but I wanted to open up the line of discarding a myr to exile or reanimate it.
- Added a small [[Entomb]] package ([[Coffin Purge]] and [[Cling to Dust]]). I worry that [[Cling to Dust]] is too powerful because once you have it in your graveyard you can get a lot of uses out of it. It's there as a test for now.
- Keeping an eye on [[Haunting Misery]]. Some players found it to be an un-fun way for the game to end. It pretty much does nothing until you just win with it. I expect that experienced players can play around it more easily, but it may be worth taking out just to remove the "oops, you're dead" aspect.
- Worried I went a little too light on the draw spells, but we will see over time.
